Invader

This image illustrates a person in a jacket positioned behind a shower door in a cozy looking bathroom with what appears to be a knife. The muted colors and shadows plays off of the audience's fear of the unknown, as the image appears to be something the mind would make up out of fear, much like hallucinations, but the weapon and figure appear to be exceptionally clear suggesting that the danger of an invader being present may be very real. The environment of the artwork also appears to take place in the bathroom, which is what many people consider to be a safe and private space. Having the artwork take place in the bathroom plays off the audience's insecurities as a place that many people consider to be a safe haven has possibly been invaded by an individual that doesn't appear to be very interested in conversing. Overall, this image plays off the audience's fear of breaches into their home, as well as the fear of their own mind betraying them and instilling unneeded fear, as the shadows and proportions of the intruder seem just off enough to suggest that it may be a hallucination that the brain has created and that the mind has betrayed the individual, or someone has truly invaded into this person's home, and both prospects suggest fairly outcomes, as the person in the artwork could be suffering with some sort of mental illness that may cause hallucinations like the artwork, or perhaps the intruder may not be alone and there may be other invaders in the person's home that we are not yet aware of.
