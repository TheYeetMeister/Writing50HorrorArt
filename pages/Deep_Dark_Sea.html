Deep Dark Sea

This image illustrates an ocean-like environment with a ladder that seems to extend to as far as the sky to as deep as the bottom of an abyss that appears to be alive, with a shark-like creature sitting at the base of the ladder appearing to block whatever attempts to cross the ladder. The muted colors disconnects the audience from the art as it doesn't allow the audience to imagine what the colors of the environment would actually be, nor does it allow the audience to easily deduce nor figure out what the creature beside the ladder is, the muted colors hides many details of the environment leaving the audience with an unknown factor about the environment. The overall image gives off a feeling of fear of the unknown as the environment appears to be unfamiliar, and the shadows near the bottom of the image appear to be alive and reaching out, making the audience wonder if the shadow of the abyss in the image is actually alive and is maybe trying to grab the creature near the ladder, this leaves the audience fearing for the creatures safety as it may be caught by the clutches of an abyss that may have a mind of its own. This artwork plays heavily on the audience's fear of the unknown, as the audience has no idea what or where this environment is, as it can be somewhere as deep as the deep dark sea, perhaps in the mountains, or in a great beyond the audience can't quite comprehend, and the encroaching darkness may possess intelligence and a will of its own.
